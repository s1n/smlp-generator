Today's Tarot for Kate Beckinsale


The Relationship spread provides insight into the interaction between two people or entities. It is the spread of choice for questions about partnerships, be they in romance or business. The Russian Tarot of Saint Petersburg is the final commissioned work of Yuri Shakov, a master in the lost art of Russian miniature painting. The rich, dark images lend themselves to questions regarding the struggle of the human spirit in trying times. The card at the top left represents how you see yourself. Three of Cups (Abundance): Resolution of a problem. Conclusion. Solace. Healing. Satisfactory result. Fulfillment. Compromise.
The card at the top right represents how you see your partner. Queen of Coins, when reversed: False prosperity. Suspense. Suspicion. Responsibilities neglected. Distrustful person. Far of failure.
The card in the center left represents how you feel about your partner. Eight of Swords (Interference), when reversed: Treachery in the past. Difficulty. Hard work. Depression. Disquiet. Accident.
The card in the center right represents what stands between you and your partner. The Moon: Deception. Twilight. Obscurity. Intuition. Disillusionment. Danger. Bad influence. Ulterior motives. False friends. Selfishness. Deceit. Craftiness. Disgrace. Slander. Libel. Superficiality. Unknown enemies.
The card in the lower left represents how your partner sees you. Queen of Cups, when reversed: Dishonesty. Unreliability. Vice.
The card in the lower right represents what your partner feels about you. Four of Swords (Truce): Respite. Rest after illness. Repose. Replenishment. Solitude. Exile. Retreat. Abandonment.
The card in the center represents the present status or challenge of the relationship. Page of Swords, when reversed: An impostor revealed. Illness is possible. Powerlessness in the face of stronger forces. Lack of preparation.

Russian Tarot of Saint Petersburg

The final commissioned work of Yuri Shakov. This deck is a masterpiece in the art of Russian miniature painting; an art rarely mastered. The rich, dark images lead themselves to more stoic interpretations.

New Palladini Tarot

A project that was 25 years in the making, David Palladini created this deck to "bridge the ancient and the future". Using elements of Medieval, Egyptian and modern art, this deck is the ideal "mirror for one's own development".

Bosch Tarot

A most unusual deck that brings to life the grotesque world of Dutch painter Hieronymus Bosch. A deck prized by both art lovers and collectors.
